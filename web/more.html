The SprintSchool Process

There's a methodology to this madness.

New Hire Crash Course

A curriculum covering an entire 3 year college program's education and beyond is condensed into an action packed 10 week crash course to provide the fundamentals needed for tech work.

Requirements Gathering & Design

Conforming to a Documentation Driven Design philosophy, all FakeCo work will start first by defining the project requirements and designing the architecture in small groups.

Execute, Release & Repeat

With the proposal signed off on, members don their assigned hats and grind through tasks as assigned by the acting Project Manager. End of month we stop, review, and start a new project.

Courses Come With Credit

Each course within the 10 week NHCC program, and any FakeCo course extensions will issue Open Badges of completion through Sertifier once the requirements are met. This integration allows members to take their achievements with them, including importing them into their LinkedIn.

Projected Schedule

The final schedule will be generated once cohort member surveys help suggest ideal session times, but for incoming members taking the New Hire Crash Course there will be a workshop each Monday, and most Wednesdays.

For members who've graduated from the New Hire path and are now engaged in FakeCo work, there will be a weekly FakeCo wide standup each Monday, as well as one workshop/week on either Monday or Wednesday depending on your cohort.

Tuesdays will be office hours and your time to grind on your assignments for the week. Thursday admins or your PM (or both) will harass you for your TPS Report. Friday Funday is just a day for random chatter/side project discussion/project pitches. Sunday we compile the TPS Digest and email it to FakeCo members along with the agenda for the following day's standup.

FakeCo work will be small group driven, with small group standup session and ad-hoc meeting scheduling to be determined by the groups.

Great Tooling = Great Experience

The education delivery stack has been carefully curated to allow for Agile curriculum expansion, immersive member engagement, and achievement certification without driving up course costs due to infrastructure fees.

Course content will be delivered and quizzed via Mini Course Generator, which is integrated with Sertifier to issue Open Badges upon course completion, allowing members to prove what they've accomplished. Workshops & Standups will take place via GoTo Meeting with sessions recorded to allow review if absent. Repos & Ops will be handled via Github Teams, leveraging Projects heavily. Office hours, meta discussions, ad-hoc voice chat/screen sharing sessions & general chatter will take place on a members only Discord. Subscriptions are handled via TillyPay, which leverages Stripe under the hood.
